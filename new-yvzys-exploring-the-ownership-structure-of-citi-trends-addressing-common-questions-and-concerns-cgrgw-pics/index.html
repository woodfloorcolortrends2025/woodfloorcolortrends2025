Citi Trends is a publicly traded company, meaning its shares are available for purchase on the stock market. Historical Context and Evolution

Citi Trends was founded in 1982 by two entrepreneurs, James E. Wilson and Robert W. Wilson. Its focus on urban and inner-city markets, combined with its understanding of the unique fashion trends and preferences of its target demographic, contributed to its early success.

However, as the company grew and expanded, it underwent a series of ownership changes, including a public offering in 1995, which allowed for broader public participation in the company’s ownership. This shift from private to public ownership marked a significant turning point in the company’s structure and governance, paving the way for the current ownership landscape.
